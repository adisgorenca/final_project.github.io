Interstellar Industries

Concepts

There are many concepts, ideas, and theories for ways to propel a spacecraft through space. Considering the vast distances needed to be traversed, the speed of each method will be stated in relation to the speed of light, which is about 670,616,629 mph. The following are methods currently being researched by Interstellar Industries.

Ion Thruster

An ion thruster or ion drive is a form of electric propulsion used for spacecraft propulsion. It creates thrust by accelerating cations by utilizing electricity. All variations have high efficiency but low thrust, reaching an average top speed of .03% the speed of light. This makes ion thrusters ideal for interplanetary journeys but too slow for interstellar trips.

Fusion Ramjet

A spacecraft using enormous electromagnetic fields (ranging from miles to many thousands of miles in diameter) as a ram scoop to collect and compress hydrogen from the interstellar medium. The hydrogen is compressed until fusion occurs in a reactor. The reactor then releases the energy and products such as protons as exhaust, propelling the spacecraft. Since it collects fuel as it goes, it has significant advantage over other designs, similar to a solar sail. Once again, it technically has infinite specific impulse, and can reach a top speed very near the speed of light. Although, such a spacecraft cannot be constructed untill the late 21 century due to technical challenges.
